About Ancient Board Games

Welcome to Ancient Board Games — your portal to the past through the timeless joy of strategic gameplay.

Our Mission

We believe that the games humans played thousands of years ago deserve to be preserved, shared, and enjoyed by modern players. These aren't just games — they're windows into ancient civilizations, their values, their strategies, and their ways of life.

Our mission is to bring these historical treasures to life with beautiful digital recreations that honor their origins while making them accessible to everyone, anywhere in the world.

The Games

We've carefully researched and recreated five of history's most significant board games:

Royal Game of Ur

Mesopotamia • 2600 BCE

One of the oldest known board games, discovered in the Royal Cemetery of Ur in modern-day Iraq.

Senet

Egypt • 3100 BCE

The game of "passing" to the afterlife, found in the tomb of Tutankhamun.

Hnefatafl

Scandinavia • 400 CE

The Viking strategy game of asymmetric warfare — can the King escape?

Nine Men's Morris

Roman Empire • 500 BCE

Carved into Roman buildings across Europe, still played today.

Mancala

Africa • 700 CE

The world's oldest family of games, with over 800 regional variations.

Historical Accuracy

We work from archaeological evidence, ancient texts, and scholarly research to recreate these games as authentically as possible. Where historical rules are uncertain or debated, we've chosen interpretations that balance authenticity with enjoyable gameplay.
